
# Security Compliance 


## Security Compliance
 : An organizations adherence to applicable security standards, regulations, and best practices to protect sensitive information, mitigate risks, and ensure data confidentiality, integrity and availability

## - Impacts of Non-Compliance

### * Fininacial Penalties
### * Disciplinary Actions
### * Lawsuits and Settlements
### * Decrease in Organizations Reputation
### * Losing Partnerships and Contracts

## Data Breach
 : When confidential or private data is read, copied, or changed without authorization


## - Consequences of Data Breaches

### * Reputational Damage
### * Identity Theft
### * Fines
### * IP Theft
 : When copywrighted material is breached

## - Notifications of Breaches

### * If there is a data breach of any kind, regardless if it did occur or it was by mistake, the proper requirements state that the parties involved must be notified

## - Impacts of Contractual Non-Compliance

### * Breach of Contract
 : One party involved in an agreement does not perform their tasks as written in the contract
### * Termination of Contract
 : The agreement between two or more parties becomes void and the partnership ends
### * Indemnification and Liability
 : Any partners who lost any assets or resources on an agreement must be compensated for their losses
### * Non-Compliance Penalties
 : Any partner who violates an existing agreement must face the consequences for not fulfilling their task


## Regulated Data
 : Information that has storage and handling compliance requirements defined by national and state legislation and/or industry regulations(HIPPA, PCI DDS, etc.)

## Trade Secrets
 : Intellectual property that gives a company a competitive advantage but hasn't been registered with a copyright, trademark, or patent

## Legal Data
 : Documents and records that relate to matters of law, such as contracts, property, court cases. and regulatory findings

## Financial Data
 : Information concering an organizations financial services, performance, and transactions including financial statements, balance sheets, income statements, cash flow statements, audit reports, tax records, financial projections, budgets, and other financial reports

## Human-Readable Data
 : Information stored in a file type that peple can access and understand using basic viewer software, such as documents, images, videos, and audio
 
## Non-Human-Readable Data
 : Information stored in a file type that human beings cannot read without a specialized processor to decode the binary or complex structure

## Data Classifications
 : The process of applying confidentiality and privacy labels to information
 * Public/Unclassified
 * Confidential/Secret
 * Critical/Top Secret


## - Alternative Types of Classification Schema

### * Proprietary
 : Information created and owned by a company
### * Private/Personal Data
 : Information that relates to an individual
### * Sensitive
 : Personal data about a subject that could harm them if made public (religious beliefs, political views, genetic data, etc.)
### * Restricted
 : Information that has strong access controls implemented and requires authorized access to view due to its confidential nature


## - Government Classifications


### * Unclassified
 : Data available for public use; no clearance needed
### * Sensitive
 : Specific data that is unclassified, but the government would perfer ifi it were not public
### * Confidential
 : Information with limited distribution; no clearance required
### * Secret
 : First data classification that requires a security clearance
### * Top Secret
 : Highest level of data classification; individuals view the information on a <ins>need to know</ins> basis
### * Tope Secret/Compartmentalized
 : Same level of security of Top Secret; has very specific and limited distribution


## Data Controllers 
 : Entities/Organizations that determines the purpose and means of processing personal data

## Data Processor
 : The entities/organizations that process datat on behalf of the data controllers

## Right to be Forgotten
 : A priciple outlined in GDPR that grants data subjects the right to request the earaser or deletion of their personal data under certain cirucumstances

## Data Inventories
 : A list of classified data or information stored or processed by a system

## Data Retention
 : The process an organization uses to maintain the existence and control over certain data in order to comply with business policies and/or applicable laws and regulations


## - Data Deconstruction Process


### * Decomissioning
 : Resetting devices back to their factory defaults and eliminating any residual settings
### * Sanitization
 : The process of removing sensitive information from storage media to prevent unauthorized access or data breaches
### * Destruction
 : The physical/electronic elimination of data stored on media, rending it inaccessible and irrecoverable
### * Certification
 : The documentation and verification of the data sanitization or destruction process
### * Overwriting
 : A process where specialized software eliminates data off of a storage device through erasing all taces of the original data on the storage unit before writing any new data
